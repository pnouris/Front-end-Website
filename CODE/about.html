Dota 2

Dota 2 is a free-to-play multiplayer online battle arena (MOBA) video game developed and published by Valve Corporation. The game is the stand-alone sequel to Defense of the Ancients (DotA). Is one of the most played games on Steam, a digital distribution platform developed by Valve Corporation.

Basic Introduction to the Game

Dota 2 is an RTS-styled competitive team game with RPG elements. Two competing teams of five players (Radiant Team and Dire Team). The main objective is to destroy the enemy Ancient inside their stronghold. Each player controls a single Hero of their choice, which can be improved during the game. By earning experience and gold when a nearby minion or heroe dies, you can upgrade your hero's abilities and buy items that provide various benefits.

Dota 2 has a heavy emphasis on tactics and team co-ordination, and a deep amount of strategy focused on building up strength as fast as possible, optimal itemization, and selecting what order to upgrade your hero's spells.

Free Content

A competitive game needs to be balanced, and to ensure that everyone is playing on an even field. Therefore, the core content of the game and all of its 113 heroes are available to all players for free. Fans can collect cosmetics for heroes and fun add-ons for the world they inhabit, but everything you need to play is already included before you join your first match.
